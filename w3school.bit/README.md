### 2.1 Engineering Perspective or Terms 
In engineering and space systems, deployable structures refer to objects that are designed to be compactly stored and later expanded or unfolded when required. These systems emphasize adaptability, efficiency, and functionality in constrained environments.

### 2.2 Software and Information Technology Perspective or terms 
In software engineering, a deployable is a finalized software artifact (such as a `.zip`, `.jar`, or executable file) that has successfully passed all development and testing stages and is ready for installation, distribution, or release to end users.
